Grammar Focus - Future Tense

Using "Going To" for Future Plans

This structure is used when you’ve already decided to do something.

Examples:
◦ I am going to visit my grandparents next weekend.
◦ She is going to study for her exams tomorrow.
◦ They are going to move to a new house.

Rule:
Use "going To" + base verb to talk about future plans or intentions.

Present Continuous for Future Arrangements

We use this tense when we’re talking about something that has already been planned or scheduled.

Examples:
◦ I’m meeting my friend for lunch tomorrow.
◦ They’re flying to Paris next week.
◦ We’re having a meeting on Friday.

Rule:
Use the present continuous
subject + am ◦ is ◦ are + -ing verb
to talk about future arrangements that are already planned or scheduled.

Vocabulary - Making Plans and Arrangements

Vocabulary for Planning and Arranging

Here are some useful phrases you can use when making plans or arrangements.

Phrases:
Book a hotel
Make a reservation
Arrange a meeting
Plan an event
Schedule an appointment

These phrases are essential when you’re organizing events, trips, or meetings.
