Introduction to Descriptive Language

Why is descriptive language important?

• Adds detail and clarity to communication
• Helps create vivid images in the listener’s or reader’s mind

Types of Descriptions:

People:
Appearance ◦ personality ◦ behaviors

Places:
Location ◦ atmosphere ◦ physical characteristics

Vocabulary

Advanced Adjectives for Describing People

Appearance:
slender ◦ graceful ◦ broad-shouldered ◦ sharp-featured

Personality:
gregarious ◦ meticulous ◦ outgoing ◦ reserved

Behavior:
attentive ◦ distracted ◦ impulsive ◦ diligent

Example Sentences:

◦ She has a graceful demeanor, always carrying herself with elegance.
◦ He’s known for being meticulous with every detail in his work.

Interactive Exercise:

Fill in the blanks:
1. He’s very __________; he loves meeting new people.

2. Her __________ nature makes her very organized and efficient.

Vocabulary

Advanced Adjectives for Describing Places

Location and Atmosphere:
picturesque ◦ bustling ◦ tranquil ◦ remote

Physical Characteristics:
sprawling ◦ cramped ◦ lush ◦ towering

Example Sentences:

◦ We visited a bustling market, full of energy and excitement.
◦ The small village was incredibly picturesque, with its charming cottages and winding streets.

Interactive Exercise:
Match the adjective with the place description:

1. Lush
2. Bustling

a) A busy city square full of people and activity
b) A tropical rainforest with dense, green vegetation

Practice Activity

Detailed Descriptions

1. Describe a person you know well using at least three advanced adjectives.

2. Describe a place you’ve visited using at least three advanced adjectives.

Wrap-Up & Homework

Homework:
Write a short descriptive paragraph about a memorable person or place you’ve encountered. Use at least five advanced adjectives.

Homework Example:

The small mountain village was so remote and picturesque that it felt like stepping into a painting. The crisp air and towering pines added to the serene atmosphere.
