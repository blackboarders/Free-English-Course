Exercise: Structuring Your Presentation

Outline a presentation on the following topic:
◦ The Impact of Technology on Education.

Introduction:
Introduce the topic and state your main thesis.

Body:
Break down the topic into 3 main points, with supporting evidence or examples.

Conclusion:
Summarize your main points and restate your thesis.

Example Outline

Introduction:
In today’s digital age, technology has become an integral part of education, revolutionizing the way students learn and teachers instruct.

Body
Access to Information:
Technology provides students with unprecedented access to information through online resources and educational platforms.

Interactive Learning:
Tools like virtual classrooms and educational apps enhance interactive learning experiences.

Challenges:
Despite its benefits, technology also poses challenges, such as digital distraction and the digital divide.

Conclusion:
Overall, while technology has significantly transformed education, it’s essential to address its challenges to maximize its benefits.

Task
Incorporate the following techniques into your presentation on:
◦ The Impact of Technology on Education.

Storytelling:
Start with a brief story or anecdote related to the topic.

Rhetorical Questions:
Pose a question to make the audience think.

Visual Aids:
Use a chart, graph, or image to illustrate a key point.

Instructions
Write out the opening: Craft the introduction of your presentation, integrating the story, rhetorical question, and a description of the visual aid you would use.

Example Answer:

Storytelling:
Imagine a classroom where every student has access to a tablet, allowing them to explore the world’s knowledge at their fingertips. This is the reality in many modern schools today, transforming how we approach education.

Rhetorical Question:
But is this access to technology truly leveling the playing field, or is it creating new challenges that we have yet to fully understand?

Visual Aid:
To illustrate this point, I will use a graph showing the correlation between technology access and student performance across different socioeconomic backgrounds, highlighting both the benefits and potential disparities.
