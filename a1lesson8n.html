Today, we are going to learn about hobbies and free time activities.
We will learn new vocabulary, practice talking about what we do in our free time, and use the simple present tense to describe our hobbies.

Let's get started!

Objectives

• Learn vocabulary related to hobbies and free time activities.
• Practice discussing what you like to do in your free time.
• Use the simple present tense to talk about hobbies.

Let’s start by learning some common words that describe different hobbies and activities you might enjoy in your free time.

He enjoys cooking.

Listening to music:
We love listening to music.

Drawing:
I enjoy drawing.

Gardening:

Simple Present Tense for Hobbies

When we talk about our hobbies, we use the simple present tense.
This tense is used for things we do regularly.

I + verb
I like cooking
You + verb
You like cooking
We + verb
We like cooking
They + verb
They like cooking
He + verb + s/es
He likes cooking
She + verb + s/es
She likes cooking
It + verb + s/es
It likes cooking

Examples:
◦ I play football every weekend.
◦ He likes painting.
◦ They watch TV in the evening.

Exercise:
Fill in the blanks with the correct form of the verb:

1. She ___ (enjoy) playing tennis.
2. I ___ (like) reading books.
3. They ___ (watch) TV every night.

Describing Your Hobbies

Now, let’s practice describing our hobbies using the simple present tense.
You can talk about what you like to do and how often you do it.

◦ I enjoy listening to music.
◦ I listen to music every day.

◦ She likes painting.
◦ She paints every weekend.

◦ They love traveling.
◦ They travel twice a year.

Exercise:
Write a few sentences about your hobbies using the simple present tense. Share your sentences with a partner or the class.

Examples:
◦ I like playing basketball.
◦ I play every Saturday.

◦ He enjoys cooking.
◦ He cooks dinner every night.

Hobbies Survey

Let’s do a hobbies survey.
You will ask your classmates about their hobbies and how often they do them.
Use the simple present tense in your questions and answers.

Questions:
What do you like to do in your free time?
How often do you play sports?
Do you enjoy reading?

Example Dialogue:

A: What do you like to do in your free time?

B: I like cycling.

A: How often do you cycle?

B: I cycle every weekend.

Exercise:
Interview three classmates about their hobbies.
Write down their answers and share them with the class.

Examples:
◦ John likes playing football.
◦ He plays every Sunday.

◦ Anna enjoys drawing.
◦ She draws every day.
