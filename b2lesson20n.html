Course Review - Key Grammar Points

Overview of Key Grammar Topics

Present Perfect Simple vs. Continuous:
I have worked here for five years.
I have been working here for five years.

Future Perfect and Continuous:
I will have finished the report by tomorrow.
I will be working on the report tomorrow.

Conditionals (First, Second, Third, and Mixed):
If you study, you will pass.
If I were you, I would study more.

Passive Voice:
The project was completed yesterday.

Modals of Probability:
She might be at home.



Examples for Practice:

1. By this time next year, I __________ (graduate) from university.

2. If I __________ (know) about the meeting, I __________ (attend).

Grammar Quiz:
Multiple-choice questions covering the main grammar points.

1. I __________ English for five years.
Options: have studied ◦ have been studying

2. If I __________ you, I __________ for the job.
Options: was ◦ were , would have applied ◦ would apply

Final Assessment

Oral and Written

Oral Assessment:

Task:
Engage in a conversation or debate on a given topic, using a range of grammar structures and vocabulary from the course.

Topics:
Discuss your future career plans.
Debate the pros and cons of social media.

Assessment Criteria:
Use of appropriate grammar
conditionals, passive voice, modals

Vocabulary usage
topic-related terms, descriptive adjectives

Communication skills
clarity, coherence, fluency

Written Assessment:

Task:
Write an essay (200-250 words) on a given topic, demonstrating mastery of the course content.

Essay Topics:
The role of technology in modern education.
The importance of maintaining a healthy lifestyle.

Assessment Criteria:
Structure
introduction, body, conclusion

Grammar accuracy
correct use of tenses, conditionals

Vocabulary richness
use of topic-specific terms, varied sentence structure
