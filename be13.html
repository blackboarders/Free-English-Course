Case Studies in Business Ethics and CSR

Let’s analyze case studies of business ethics and CSR in action.

I’ll present scenarios where ethical decisions or CSR initiatives had a significant impact on business reputation and outcomes.

We’ll discuss the ethical considerations and lessons learned from these cases.

Case Study: Johnson & Johnson and the Tylenol Crisis

Scenario:

In 1982, Johnson & Johnson faced a major crisis when several people died after consuming Tylenol capsules that had been tampered with and laced with cyanide. The company faced an ethical dilemma: how to respond to the crisis in a way that protected public safety while maintaining the company's reputation and trust.

Johnson & Johnson's response was swift and transparent. They immediately recalled 31 million bottles of Tylenol, despite the significant financial cost. The company also cooperated fully with law enforcement, launched an extensive public relations campaign to inform the public, and introduced tamper-resistant packaging. This proactive and ethical response not only helped to save lives but also restored public confidence in the brand.

Ethical Considerations:

Public Safety:
Johnson & Johnson prioritized the health and safety of its customers over financial considerations.

Transparency:
The company communicated openly and honestly with the public and authorities.

Accountability:
Johnson & Johnson took full responsibility for addressing the crisis and preventing future occurrences.

Innovation in Safety:
The introduction of tamper-resistant packaging set new industry standards and demonstrated a commitment to preventing future tampering incidents.

Lessons Learned:

Prioritizing Ethics Over Profits:
The company’s decision to recall Tylenol, despite the cost, showed a strong commitment to ethical principles and public safety.

Building Trust Through Transparency:
Open communication during the crisis helped maintain and restore public trust.

Long-Term Reputation Management:
Ethical decision-making during a crisis can enhance a company's long-term reputation and customer loyalty.

Proactive Measures:
Introducing tamper-resistant packaging showed a forward-thinking approach to ensuring customer safety and set new industry standards.

Discussion Points:

Q. How did Johnson & Johnson's response to the Tylenol crisis demonstrate ethical leadership?

Johnson & Johnson’s response was characterized by prioritizing customer safety, transparency, and accountability. This approach reflected a strong ethical foundation and leadership.

Q. What role did transparency play in managing the crisis?

Transparency was crucial in maintaining public trust. By openly communicating the situation and the steps being taken to address it, Johnson & Johnson reassured the public and demonstrated their commitment to resolving the issue.

Q. How did the introduction of tamper-resistant packaging contribute to the company’s CSR initiatives?

The tamper-resistant packaging was a proactive measure that went beyond the immediate crisis response. It contributed to public safety and set a new industry standard, showcasing Johnson & Johnson's commitment to preventing future incidents and enhancing consumer protection.

Q. What are the long-term benefits of ethical decision-making during a crisis?

Ethical decision-making can lead to enhanced reputation, increased customer loyalty, and trust. It can also set a positive example for the industry and contribute to long-term success by aligning business practices with societal values.
