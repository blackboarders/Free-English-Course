Grammar Focus

Present Perfect Tense

Form:
Have ◦ Has + past participle

Examples:
◦ I have been to Paris.
◦ She has seen that movie.

Usage:
To talk about experiences at an unspecified time in the past.

We use have or has followed by the past participle of the verb.

The present perfect is used to talk about experiences at some point in the past without specifying exactly when they happened.

Grammar Focus

Common Past Participles

Examples:
Go → gone/been
See → seen
Do → done
Visit → visited
Eat → eaten

For regular verbs, you just add -ed, like visit becomes visited.

For irregular verbs, the past participle is often different.
Example: go becomes gone

It’s important to remember these irregular forms for the present perfect tense.

Vocabulary

Phrases for Describing Experience

Here are some useful phrases for describing experiences.

Examples:
◦ I have traveled to…
◦ She has tried…
◦ They have visited…
◦ He has never…

These phrases are great for talking about your past experiences.

Practice - Present Perfect Tense

Complete the sentences using the present perfect tense:

1. I (visit) __________ many countries.
2. She (try) __________ sushi.
3. They (never/go) __________ skiing.

Practice - Talking About Experiences

Pair up with a classmate and ask each other about your experiences using the present perfect tense. Use questions like:

1. Have you ever been to another country?
2. What new foods have you tried?
3. Have you ever done something adventurous?
