Grammar Focus

Modal Verbs for Advice

We use should, must, and ought to when we want to suggest something to someone.

Examples:
◦ You should eat more vegetables.
◦ You must exercise regularly.
◦ You ought to drink more water.

Usage:

Should:
Used for giving general advice.

Must:
Used for strong recommendations or obligations.

Ought to:
Used for giving advice, similar to should.

Vocabulary

Health and Fitness

Examples:

Diet:
Balanced diet, nutrition, healthy eating

Exercise:
Workout, cardio, strength training

Health:
Mental health, physical health, well-being

Practice - Using Modal Verbs for Advice

Giving Health Advice

Activity:
Complete the sentences with the correct modal verb:

1. You __________ (should/must) get at least 8 hours of sleep every night.
2. You __________ (should/must) drink more water if you feel tired.
3. You __________ (ought to/should) see a doctor if the pain continues.

Practice - Giving Health Advice Role-Play

In pairs, give advice to your partner on staying healthy.
Use modal verbs like should, must, and ought to.
Some scenarios include:

1. Someone wants to lose weight.
2. Someone feels stressed and needs to relax.
3. Someone wants to improve their fitness level.
